Fashion that refuses to be background noise — built for the kind of person who stops traffic.
Obsidian Apparel needed a launch campaign that positioned them immediately at the intersection of fashion and art. No photography. All illustration. The directive: cinematic, narrative, stop-scroll. The result was a six-piece editorial series that earned features in Hypebeast and Highsnobiety within ten days of release.

Process

Editorial as Narrative

Concept Development
The starting point was a single sentence from the creative brief: "dress like the last person at the end of the world who still cares." From that, a visual language emerged — structural, formal, a little melancholic. Illustration over photography because it allowed us to build unreality.
Illustration Direction
Six editorial spreads, each a distinct setting within the same collapsed world. Every figure was composed like a painting — deliberate proportions, atmospheric tonal work, clothing treated as sculpture. The campaign reads like a gallery, not an advert.
Digital Release Strategy
Released across 3 weeks in drops of two images each, with accompanying editorial text. The drip approach generated anticipation and kept the brand in conversation across two consecutive news cycles in both Hypebeast and Highsnobiety.
